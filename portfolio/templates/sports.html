Beyond the Screen

When I'm not coding,
I'm on the track.

I'm a national-level track athlete. Running is where I learned everything I know about discipline, patience, and iteration — the same principles I bring to every project I build.

Track and field taught me something that no course ever could: the gap between where you are and where you want to be is filled by showing up every single day, even when progress is invisible.

Sprint training is a science — biomechanics, muscle fiber types, energy systems, recovery windows. Being an athlete is actually what pulled me toward biology in the first place. Once I realized I could model my own movement with computer vision, the AI Biomechanics project basically wrote itself.

I think being an athlete makes me a better builder. You learn to be honest with yourself about your current level. You learn that rest is part of the work. And you learn that the only real competition is the person you were yesterday.
